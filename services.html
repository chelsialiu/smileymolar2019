Bonding

Bonding is done to repair a damaged tooth, alter the alignment of a tooth, close gaps between teeth, or for cosmetic purposes. It involves adhering composite resin material that is matched to the color of the front of the tooth, blending in easily with the rest of your smile!

Bridges

It is important that a missing tooth be replaced as soon as possible to prevent deteriorating teeth and gum disease. Using the neighboring teeth as support, a bridge can be formed to look like the missing tooth and replace it both functionally and cosmetically.

Crowns

Crowns are full coverage restorations that are used to cover a tooth that is likely to break or is too broken down to be restored with a filling. They are most commonly done after root canal treatments or when a large filling wears out.

Dentures

Dentures replace loose or missing tooth due to bone loss. We know no one enjoys losing their natural teeth, but dentures will help you eat and talk regularly, providing all the normal functionality and appearance of your natural smile.

Digital X-Rays

X-rays are a necessary part of the diagnostic process. Using digital radiography, we can better diagnosis any dental concerns and our patients are exposed to 50-70% less radiation than conventional X-rays.

Fillings

Fillings are done to remove decay and replace the affected tooth structure. Once the decay is removed, material is used to 'fill' the hole in the tooth easily and painlessly, restoring full functionality to the tooth.

Laughing Gas (Nitrous Oxide)

Laughing gas is not really a service in and of itself, but it is used to make other treatments more comfortable. It is safe, effective, and pleasant anesthetic that will make your appointment more enjoyable.

Gum Treatment

If you have gums that are red and bleed easily, persistent bad breath, gums that are pulled away from the tooth, loose teeth, or changes in your bite, these cann all jeopardize the foundation for your teeth. Appropriate gum treatments can return that foundation to a healthy state.

Root Canals

Root canals are necessary when a cavity infects the tooth pulp. If left untreated, the infection can be dangerous and painful. Once the infection is resolved, the canal is filled in to prevent further infection.

Sealants

Sealant is painted on the chewing surfaces of teeth to prevent tooth decay. With a sealant coating, the grooves and pits of a tooth are evened out and a protective shield covers each tooth.

Tooth Implants

Implants recreate a tooth root within the bone of your mouth. They can effectively replace a single tooth with a crown, multiple teeth with a bridge, or all upper or lower teeth with dentures.
